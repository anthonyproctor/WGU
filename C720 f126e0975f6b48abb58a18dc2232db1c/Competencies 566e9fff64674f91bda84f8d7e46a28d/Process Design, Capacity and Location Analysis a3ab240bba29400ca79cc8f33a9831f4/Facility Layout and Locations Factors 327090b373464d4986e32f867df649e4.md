# Facility Layout and Locations Factors

- Product oriented layout - is characterized by high demand for the same or similar products. Also called sequential layout.
    - Examples include refining steel, making paper, processing check in a bank

- Process oriented layout - is characterized by the production of many different products with the same equipment and low volume of any individual product. Also called Functional Layout.
    - Examples include a machine shop that produces specialty automotive parts for racing engines, a hospital emergency room, and an automotive repair ship that offers a wide variety of services.
